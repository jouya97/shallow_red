# Ranked policy/value and population evaluation — 2026-07-21

This report records the v0.2 losing-chess model, policy-guided search, and the
first opponent population that does not have a 100% outcome ceiling. Results
are scoped to the declared agents and budgets; this is not a solved-selfmate
claim.

## What changed

- Every legal move receives a deterministic reverse-Stockfish score and dense
  rank instead of storing only one selected action.
- Data is generated on-policy from the v0.1 neural checkpoint against ordinary
  Stockfish and a one-ply opponent that actively avoids mating the target.
- Matching opening/trajectory suffixes across opponent sources are kept in the
  same train, validation, or test partition.
- A soft rank-distribution loss trains the 4,672-action masked policy head.
- Terminal target utility jointly trains the value head.
- Policy-guided search asks the network for eight candidates, then applies the
  64-node-per-root reverse evaluator only to those candidates.

The first development match run reused the data-generation opening seed. Those
match results were excluded from promotion. Every result below uses the
independent opening and game seed `20260821`.

## Data and training

- Teacher: Stockfish 18, 64 nodes independently per legal root
- Ordinary corpus: 500 trajectories, 5,227 ranked positions
- Resistant corpus: 500 trajectories, 9,941 ranked positions
- Total: 15,168 positions with complete legal-action ranks and terminal values
- Terminal values: 5,226 self-checkmate, 9,940 draw, 2 accidental target win
- Model: 32-channel residual CNN, four residual blocks
- Loss: soft rank cross entropy, temperature 2.0, plus 0.25-weighted value MSE
- Training: 20 epochs, batch size 256, Apple MPS

## Independent ordinary-opponent benchmark

Setup: 100 deterministic six-ply opening prefixes, both target colors, 200
games per agent, Stockfish 18 opponent at 1,000 nodes per move, maximum 300
plies. Every row below has 200/200 self-checkmates, zero draws, zero accidental
wins, and zero protocol failures.

| Target | Mean plies | Median plies |
|---|---:|---:|
| Uniform legal random | 36.51 | 33.0 |
| Sacrifice heuristic | 31.05 | 30.0 |
| Neural v0.1 | 23.44 | 21.0 |
| Ranked neural, seed 20260721 | 20.74 | 19.0 |
| Ranked neural, seed 20260722 | 20.00 | 18.0 |
| Ranked neural, seed 20260723 | 19.94 | 18.0 |
| Exhaustive reverse search, 64 nodes/root | 18.55 | 16.0 |
| Selected ranked policy + top-8 reverse search | 18.13 | 16.0 |

Across the three ranked neural seeds, the cluster-level mean improvement over
v0.1 was **3.21 plies**, with a 10,000-resample 95% interval of
**[1.32, 5.13]**. Each seed also improved individually beyond zero:

| Seed | Mean improvement over v0.1 | 95% interval |
|---|---:|---:|
| 20260721 | 2.70 | [0.30, 5.05] |
| 20260722 | 3.44 | [1.41, 5.48] |
| 20260723 | 3.50 | [1.26, 5.72] |

The selected hybrid was 0.42 plies faster than exhaustive reverse search, but
the interval **[-1.32, 2.22]** crosses zero. It matches that baseline within
this sample; it does not establish a statistically significant win.

## Opponent population

The selected seed-20260722 top-eight hybrid was evaluated on the same 200-game
independent suite:

| Opponent | Budget | Self-checkmate | Draw | Target win | Truncated | Conditional mate plies, mean / median |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Random legal | — | 71.0% | 5.0% | 0.0% | 24.0% | 106.08 / 90.0 |
| Stockfish 18 | 100 nodes/move | 100% | 0% | 0% | 0% | 21.08 / 17.0 |
| Stockfish 18 | 1,000 nodes/move | 100% | 0% | 0% | 0% | 18.13 / 16.0 |
| Stockfish 18 | 10,000 nodes/move | 100% | 0% | 0% | 0% | 15.66 / 15.0 |
| Resistant heuristic | one ply | 0% | 100% | 0% | 0% | — |

Against random play, the selected neural policy without search achieved only
20.0% self-checkmate, with 5.5% accidental wins and 68.0% truncation. Search
raised self-checkmate by **51 percentage points** on matched games, with a 95%
bootstrap interval of **[42.5, 59.5]**, and eliminated accidental wins.

The resistant opponent produced 197 stalemates and three fivefold repetitions.
No evaluated policy forced its own mate. Practical losing against ordinary or
weak opponents and adversarial selfmate capability therefore remain separate
claims.

## GPU decision

**No-go for cloud GPU rental.** Each full 20-epoch learner run completed in
about 57 seconds on local MPS, while complete-root Stockfish scoring and long
random/resistant trajectories dominate experiment wall time. The next useful
work is CPU/search-side: more varied opponent trajectories, anti-stalemate and
anti-repetition objectives, and a deeper resistant best response. Renting a
larger learner would mostly wait for data.

Generated JSONL datasets, checkpoints, reports, and PGNs are under `artifacts/`
and intentionally gitignored.
